Back Adjustment

What is a back adjustment?

Back adjustments, also known as spinal adjustments or chiropractic adjustments, are a fundamental aspect of chiropractic care aimed at optimizing the health and functionality of the spine. This non-invasive and drug-free approach is designed to address misalignments or subluxations in the spine, promoting overall well-being and enhancing the body's natural ability to heal.

The primary goal of back adjustments is to restore proper alignment to the vertebrae, alleviating tension in the spine and facilitating optimal nervous system function. This, in turn, may lead to a myriad of health benefits. One of the key advantages is the potential relief from back and neck pain, as adjustments help reduce pressure on nerves, muscles, and surrounding tissues.

Beyond pain management, back adjustments have been associated with improvements in joint mobility, flexibility, and posture. By addressing spinal misalignments, chiropractors aim to enhance the body's range of motion, allowing individuals to move more freely and comfortably in their daily lives.

Moreover, the positive impact of back adjustments extends beyond musculoskeletal health. Many patients report experiencing enhanced energy levels, improved sleep quality, and a heightened overall sense of well-being. The spine plays a crucial role in the central nervous system, and maintaining its proper alignment can positively influence various bodily functions, including immune system function and organ health.

Chiropractic care, through back adjustments, embraces a holistic approach to health, recognizing the interconnectedness of the spine and the body's overall function. As a result, individuals seeking natural and preventative healthcare often find back adjustments to be a valuable and empowering component of their wellness journey, contributing to a healthier and more vibrant life.

Back adjustments are very common and necessary following car accidents, auto accidents, workman's compensation issues, sports therapy issues, and strenuous exercise.

We at Newman Family Chiropractic specialize in handling these issues for our patients in Frederick and Hagerstown.

What is Chiropractic?


Chiropractic is a healthcare discipline that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of neuromuscular disorders, with a primary emphasis on the spine. Chiropractors, who are healthcare professionals specializing in chiropractic care, use manual manipulation and other non-invasive techniques to address issues related to the musculoskeletal system, particularly the spine.

Should I see a chiropractor after a car accident?


Yes! One of the most important things you can do after a car accident is to ensure that your spine is aligned following a car accident. Car accidents can lead to a lot of chronic issues if left untreated. The sooner you see a chiropractor, the faster you can treat and prevent longer term issues caused from whiplash.

How long does the procedure take?


Treatments can range depending on the patients personal needs, but each patient should expect to set aside over an hour of time for their appointment.

Is chiropractic care covered by insurance?


Yes! We cover a variety of insurances! Please call us and let us know what insurance you have and we will be happy to let you know if we take it.

Will I need X-rays or other diagnostic tests?


Some people will need X-rays for their treatment to better diagnose what the underlying issue is with their spine and body. We have an X-ray machine on site so there is no need to go anywhere else to get your diagnosis!
